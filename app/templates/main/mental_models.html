The Research Workflow

A systematic path from initial idea to long-term monitoring using powerful mental models.

1. Foundational Analysis: The Checklist

Inspired by Charlie Munger

The starting point for all disciplined research. A checklist enforces rigor and ensures all key quantitative and qualitative aspects of a business are thoroughly investigated before forming an initial thesis. It is your primary defense against emotional decision-making and errors of omission.

Go to Checklists
How to Use It (Example)

For a company like Tesla (TSLA), a checklist item might be:

"Assess dependence on key suppliers for battery components."

Your research would then document the risks and relationships associated with Panasonic, CATL, etc.

2. Strategic Frameworks: SWOT & Porter's

Classic Business Strategy

After initial data gathering, use these models to understand the company's strategic position (SWOT) and the competitive dynamics of its industry (Porter's Five Forces). This builds a deeper, qualitative understanding around the numbers.

Analyze a Company
How to Use It (Example)

For a company like Apple (AAPL):

  • Strength (SWOT): Its powerful brand and closed ecosystem.
  • Threat (SWOT): Increasing antitrust regulation globally.
  • Bargaining Power of Buyers (Porter's): Relatively low, due to high switching costs and brand loyalty.

3. Long-Term Monitoring: Destination Analysis

Inspired by Nick Sleep & Qais Zakaria

For companies in your portfolio or on your watchlist. Instead of focusing on the next quarter, you set multi-year "checkpoints" for your thesis. This model forces you to think about a company's enduring competitive advantages and long-term trajectory.

View Checkpoints Timeline
How to Use It (Example)

For a company like Amazon (AMZN), a 2-year checkpoint might be:

  • Metric: 'AWS Revenue Growth.'
  • Expectation: 'Maintain >25% year-over-year growth.'

You would then review this in two years to see if your thesis is on track.

4. Continuous Learning: Scuttlebutt & Mistake Log

Inspired by Philip Fisher & Charlie Munger

Investing is a process of constant learning. Use the Digital Scuttlebutt tool to stay on top of the current narrative, and the Mistake Log to permanently embed lessons from your own and others' failures into your future process.

Go to Mistake Log
How to Use It (Example)

For Digital Scuttlebutt, the AI might analyze news for NVIDIA (NVDA) and find a Bear Case: "New export restrictions to China."

For the Mistake Log, you might log the lesson: "Avoid companies with high customer concentration." and then click a button to add this permanently to your "Initial Screening" checklist.
